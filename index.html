Women in STEM at St. John's University

An Oragnization Built by Women for Women in the STEM Sector

Although comprising 50% of the US workforce, women only make up about 27% of STEM workers. A multitude of factors has contributed to this low turnout rate—primarily misogyny embedded into our culture. Fields such as that of STEM are often stereotyped as male domains. A lack of female representation in these roles strays other young women from believing that this is something they can partake in. Young women see few women going into science, technology, engineering, and math fields, so they have fewer role models and examples to follow. Despite this, the women that do end up becoming medical providers, software engineers, physicists, and so on, report experiencing adversaries in their workplace—often including sexual harassment, belittlement, and an overall un-welcoming community. The purpose of the Women in STEM club is to share the diversity within the science, technology, engineering, and mathematics fields. We wish to promote the contributions women have made to the STEM fields, while encouraging more contributions in the future, from our members.

Giving Back

We want our impact to go past the walls of St. John's. UNICEF reports that 129 million girls do not have access to education, and only 49 percent of countries have achieved gender parity in primary education. WIS is determined to do everything in our abilities to provide these women with educational resources through charity events.
