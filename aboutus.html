Get To Know The Board

Teuta Elezaj - President and Founder

Hi everyone! Thank you so much for checking out WIS SJU. My name is Teuta Elezaj and I am a rising senior majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Mathematics. My parents immigrated here from Kosovo, making me a first generation American and college student. I originally enrolled into SJU as a biomedicine major, but, long story short, realized it wasn’t the path for me. It took me about a year and a half to have the guts to change my path, now that I have been a CS student for awhile, I couldn’t be happier with my decision. For a longer excerpt of my story, check out my blog post here. I have previously interned as a SWE and analyst, and am also the president of SJU ACM. Being that I have been in a couple of different STEM fields, and in high school I was also in a STEM program, I've been around men for the vast majority of my life and have been confronted with several different instances of misogynistic conduct and comments. I hope that this club can serve as a safe space for you all to thrive and succeed, and I want us to create an environment where motivation is a permanent feeling. My current career goal is to be a software engineer, and in the future I hope to integrate my passion for business with my coding abilities and have my very own grossly overvalued start-up (haha). Weightlifting is a huge passion of mine and in my free time, I'm usually working on a coding project (like this website!) or watching Survivor.

Reyan Al Taee - Vice President

Hey everyone! my name is Reyan and I’m a junior majoring in BioMedical Sciences. I got into St. John’s as a pharmacy major but while working at a pharmacy I realized I wanted to know beyond that this patient will be receiving this medication. I wanted to interact with patients and know what exactly is this drug doing to their system and if its not working what alternatives are available. I am also the public relations chair of the badminton club. I am currently working with professor Charles Fortmann, who is a physics professor that graduated from Stanford, teaching little kids physics at a school close to campus and soon will be working on his research that is making essential products from scratch that will be helping Puerto Rico to function day to day activities. I’m also pursuing a minor in psychology and that is mainly because I’m very intrigued by how the mind works. In all the extracurricular activities that I do, one thing I realized is the huge gap between men and women participation. I hope to see an increase in women in the research programs and so on. I love languages. I speak three languages and I am currently trying to learn Turkish. I love watching romance shows like Turkish series and k-dramas. I also love reading books, fiction as well as nonfiction. I love watching sunrises and sunsets and going on long drives alone. P.S. sunrises are prettier and calmer than sunsets!
